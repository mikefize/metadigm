WRITING STYLE: Gritty & Grounded.
Write like a modern thriller author (e.g., Gillian Flynn or Chuck Palahniuk). Sentences should be sharp and punchy. Focus on the raw, uncomfortable reality of the situation. Describe the sweat, the ache in the muscles, the panic in the chest. Avoid all poetic or flowery language. Do not sound like a doctor; describe feelings as a person experiences them (e.g., "Her heart hammered," not "Her adrenaline spiked").

Prioritize a casual, conversational tone that feels immediate and intimate, as if the narrator is speaking directly to the reader. The mood is visceral and grounded, with a touch of absurdity or irony to heighten the realism of the moment. Use short, punchy sentences and fragmented thoughts to create a sense of urgency or disorientation, mirroring the character’s experience. Sentences often begin abruptly, with minimal transition, to pull the reader into the scene without warning. The diction is colloquial and modern, sprinkled with contractions (“wanna,” “couldn’t”) and informal phrasing to reflect natural speech. Avoid overly poetic or archaic language; instead, lean into raw, unfiltered reactions to make the prose feel authentic and alive.
For pacing, alternate between rapid-fire exchanges and introspective pauses. Use staccato rhythms in dialogue or action sequences to quicken the tempo, then slow down for moments of reflection or sensory detail. This contrast keeps the reader engaged and mimics the ebb and flow of real-life perception. When describing surroundings or internal states, favor specific, concrete imagery over abstract metaphors—ground the reader in tangible sensations (e.g., “the world had finally stopped spinning”) rather than vague emotions. The goal is to immerse the reader in the immediacy of the experience, making them feel as though they are living the moment alongside the narrator.
